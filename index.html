Partnership with USADF

Through 14 Cooperative Agreements (CAs) with the United States African Development Foundation (USADF), ADC has supported MSMEs and more than 100 agricultural cooperatives in project development, enabling them to access grants and, in some cases, secure follow-on financing from financial institutions. Well-versed in managing a diverse portfolio, ADC has further implemented Power Africa programs to advance USADF Off-Grid Energy Initiatives; Implemented the Young African Leaders Initiative (YALI) and the Academy for Women Entrepreneurs (AWE), not only in accessing grants, but also in capacity building and monitoring services.

Our Impact

Developed business plans on behalf of Rwanda Dairy Development Project (RDDP) beneficiaries, enabling more than 50 smallholder farmers to access financial services through matching grants. Prepared business plans for Post-Harvest and Agribusiness Support Project (PASP) beneficiaries, facilitating access to financial services for over 85 agribusinesses. Supported the Project for Rural Income through Exports (PRICE) by developing business plans that helped more than 600 smallholder farmers gain access to financial services.

East Africa & Great Lakes Region

ADC operates across five countries in the East Africa and Great Lakes region, with a deep portfolio in agribusiness, renewable energy, and MSME finance. Our regional presence allows us to understand local contexts while leveraging cross-border opportunities.

Countries of Operation

  • Rwanda - Headquarters and primary operations hub
  • Burundi - Agricultural development and cooperative support
  • Uganda - Youth entrepreneurship and financial inclusion
  • Tanzania - Market development and value chain integration
  • Democratic Republic of Congo - Capacity building and institutional support
